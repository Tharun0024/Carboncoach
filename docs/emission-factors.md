This document outlines the emission factors used in CarbonCoach to calculate CO₂ equivalent (CO₂e) emissions. These factors are based on global averages from reputable sources and may vary by region and specific circumstances.

### Data Sources
The values listed above are derived and aggregated from the following primary sources:

- **IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6) 2022:** Provides comprehensive data on global greenhouse gas emissions across all sectors.
- **US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA):** The EPA's Emission Factors Hub offers detailed factors for a wide range of activities.
- **Our World In Data:** A project of the Global Change Data Lab, providing data and research on major global challenges, including climate change and emissions.

### Disclaimer
Emission factors are subject to significant variation based on geographical location, technology, and methodology. For example, the carbon intensity of electricity depends heavily on the local energy grid's mix (renewables vs. fossil fuels). The factors used in CarbonCoach represent global averages and are intended to provide a reasonable estimate for coaching purposes, not for precise scientific or compliance reporting.
